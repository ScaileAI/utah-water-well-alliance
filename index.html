Utah Well Permit Guide

How to Get a Water Well Permit in Utah

Navigating the Utah Division of Water Rights process can be confusing. We've broken it down into clear steps — and we can handle most of it for you.

1

Verify Your Water Right

Search the Utah DWRi database to check existing rights on your parcel before applying.

2

Apply for a Water Right

File an "Application to Appropriate Water" if no right exists. Domestic use ≤0.5 acre-feet may qualify for an expedited domestic exemption.

3

File Your Well Permit Application

Submit a well permit application through the Utah Division of Water Rights online portal with your licensed driller's information.

4

Receive Your Start Card

Once approved, you receive your Authorization to Construct (Start Card) — this must be posted at your drill site. Drilling cannot begin without it.

5

Drill & File Driller's Report

Your licensed driller completes the well and files a Driller's Report with the state within 30 days of completion.

Read the Full Guide

Important Utah Well Facts

  • All wells must be drilled by a Utah State licensed well driller
  • The Start Card (Authorization to Construct) must be posted on-site before drilling begins
  • Domestic wells (≤0.5 acre-feet/year) may qualify for a simplified domestic exemption
  • Well permits typically cost $75–$150 in state fees
  • Start Cards are valid for 2 years from date of issue
  • A Driller's Report must be filed within 30 days of well completion
  • Wells must be set back at least 100 feet from septic systems and 50 feet from property lines
  • Washington County aquifers are heavily managed — apply early
