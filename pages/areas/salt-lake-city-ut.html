Local Knowledge

Salt Lake County Geology & Well Considerations

Understanding Salt Lake County's hydrology is key to a successful well. Here's what property owners need to know.

  • Jordan Valley Aquifer: The primary groundwater source for rural Salt Lake County — a deep alluvial aquifer fed by Wasatch Front streams. Well depths typically range from 200–600 feet depending on location.
  • Municipal water areas: Most Salt Lake City proper and suburban areas are served by municipal water. Private wells are most common in rural areas, foothill parcels, and properties west of the Oquirrh Mountains.
  • Water right competition: Salt Lake County is one of the most over-appropriated water basins in Utah. Plan for longer permit review times and work with experts who know the system.
  • Wasatch Front geology: Foothill properties often encounter consolidated granite and quartzite requiring percussion or rotary drilling. Valley floor properties drill through gravel and sand alluvial deposits.
  • Water quality: Test for TDS, hardness, and nitrates — common in Salt Lake County agricultural areas. Industrial history in some parts of the county makes testing essential before use.
