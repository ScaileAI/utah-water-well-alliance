Price & Carbon County Well Considerations

Carbon County's coal mining heritage and Price River valley geology create specific well drilling considerations.

  • Price River Alluvial Aquifer: The primary productive zone near Price City. Wells typically reach groundwater at 200–400 feet in the valley floor.
  • Water quality testing: Carbon County's mining history makes water quality testing essential. Test for sulfates, iron, TDS, and any mining-related contaminants before using well water.
  • Sandstone uplands: Properties outside the valley floor encounter consolidated Wasatch and Green River Formation sandstone, requiring air-rotary or percussion drilling techniques.
