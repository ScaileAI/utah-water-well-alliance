Local Knowledge

Ogden & Weber County Geology & Well Considerations

Weber County's geology ranges from mountain bedrock to fertile valley alluvium. Key facts for well owners.

  • Weber Valley Aquifer: The primary groundwater source — an alluvial aquifer fed by the Weber River and Ogden River. Well depths typically 150–450 feet in the valley.
  • Ogden Valley (Eden/Huntsville): Mountain valley properties have shallower water tables but require drilling through consolidated volcanic and sedimentary rock in some areas.
  • Agricultural wells: West Weber County has significant agricultural use. Irrigation wells often require separate water rights applications beyond the domestic exemption.
  • Water hardness: Weber County groundwater tends to be hard due to calcium carbonate in the aquifer. A softener system is often recommended after drilling.
