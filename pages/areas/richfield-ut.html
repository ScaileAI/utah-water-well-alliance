Richfield & Sevier County Well Considerations

Key facts for Sevier County property owners planning to drill a well.

  • Sevier Valley Aquifer: A productive alluvial aquifer in the Sevier River valley supplies groundwater at 150–500 feet. This is one of Utah's more accessible and productive well areas for rural properties.
  • Agricultural irrigation: Many Sevier County properties have existing irrigation rights tied to the Sevier River. If you also need a domestic well, a separate domestic water right application is typically required.
  • Upland geology: Properties on the Fishlake Plateau, Monroe Mountain, or East Fork Sevier Canyon areas encounter volcanic and sedimentary rock requiring air-rotary drilling and deeper wells.
