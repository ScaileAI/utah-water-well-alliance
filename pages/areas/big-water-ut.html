Water Well Drilling & Pump Services in Big Water, Utah

Utah Water Well Alliance serves Big Water and the surrounding Lake Powell–Glen Canyon area of southern Kane County with professional water well drilling, pump installation, and 24/7 emergency response. One of the most remote communities in Utah, Big Water relies almost exclusively on deep private wells where municipal water infrastructure is essentially nonexistent — making expert well construction and reliable pump service absolutely critical.

Big Water, Kane County

Lake Powell Area Well Specialists

Big Water sits at the southern tip of Kane County near the Utah–Arizona border, on the edge of the Glen Canyon National Recreation Area. The geology here is dominated by massive Navajo Sandstone — the same deep red formation that creates the iconic canyon country landscape — underlain by older Jurassic and Triassic formations. Reaching productive water in this fractured rock environment typically requires drilling to depths of 500 to 900 feet or more, with water found in fracture systems and porous sandstone zones far below the desert surface.

The extreme remoteness of Big Water creates unique logistical challenges for well drilling and service. Equipment mobilization, supply staging, and crew logistics require careful planning. At the same time, water quality concerns are significant in this area — elevated iron, total dissolved solids (TDS), and other minerals are common in deep desert aquifers. Utah Water Well Alliance performs comprehensive water quality testing on every completed well and can recommend appropriate treatment systems for the specific chemistry found in Big Water area wells.

Local Knowledge

What Drillers Need to Know About Big Water

  • ▶Navajo Sandstone requires deep drilling with precise fracture targeting. The Navajo Sandstone formation that underlies most of the Big Water area is highly variable — sections can be tight and nearly impermeable, while fracture zones can yield significant water. Successful wells here depend on drilling deep enough to intercept productive fractures, and may require hydraulic fracturing or well development to maximize yield. Depths of 700–900+ feet are not unusual.
  • ▶Water quality testing is mandatory — not optional. Iron and TDS levels in Big Water area wells frequently exceed EPA secondary standards without treatment. Before connecting a new well to a household or livestock supply, comprehensive laboratory testing for iron, manganese, TDS, hardness, arsenic, and bacteria is essential. We include full water quality analysis with every new well we complete in this area.
  • ▶Remote logistics require advance planning for service calls. Big Water is over 70 miles from Kanab and more than 100 miles from most major supply points. Well service calls require careful parts staging and equipment preparation — we cannot make a quick return trip for a missing component. Our crews mobilizing to Big Water arrive fully equipped for the job, reducing downtime for property owners in this isolated community.
