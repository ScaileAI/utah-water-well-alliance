Toquerville Well Drilling & Service

Toquerville is one of Washington County's quieter communities — a rural town perched above the Virgin River with views of Zion's red cliffs and a landscape shaped by the Hurricane Fault. Properties here tend to be larger, with many homeowners and small agricultural operations depending entirely on private well water for domestic use and livestock.

The geology around Toquerville reflects its position near the Hurricane Fault zone — volcanic intrusions, fault-offset sandstone, and river alluvium can all be encountered at depth. Well conditions vary significantly depending on which side of the fault system a property sits on, making a thorough pre-drill assessment essential. Utah Water Well Alliance has extensive experience drilling in this corridor.

Local Knowledge

Toquerville Well Facts

  • Hurricane Fault proximity: Toquerville sits near the Hurricane Fault scarp — one of Utah's most significant fault systems. This dramatically offsets rock formations, meaning subsurface conditions can change over short horizontal distances. Local drilling expertise is essential.
  • Volcanic bench terrain: The bench areas around Toquerville include lava-capped formations similar to those found near La Verkin and Hurricane. Air-hammer drilling equipment is required to penetrate volcanic rock effectively.
  • Rural property sizes: Many Toquerville properties have acreage that qualifies for the domestic exemption (up to 0.5 acre-feet per year). We help property owners determine the right permit pathway — domestic exemption vs. full water right application — before the project starts.
