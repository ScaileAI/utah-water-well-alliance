Escalante, Garfield County

Escalante & Grand Staircase Area Well Specialists

Escalante sits at the heart of Garfield County — one of Utah's most spectacular and remote areas, surrounded by Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument, Capitol Reef, and Bryce Canyon. It's also one of the most geologically complex parts of Southern Utah.

Wells in the Escalante area typically penetrate thick sequences of Navajo Sandstone, Entrada Sandstone, and underlying Kayenta Formation. Water-bearing zones can be found at variable depths depending on exact location and proximity to natural fracture systems. A thorough site assessment and review of nearby well log data is essential before committing to a drill location in this area.

Local Knowledge

Escalante & Garfield County Well Facts

  • Fractured rock aquifers: Unlike basin aquifers, Escalante-area wells often rely on water stored in fracture systems within sandstone formations. Well productivity depends heavily on intersecting productive fracture zones — making site selection critical.
  • Access coordination: Many Garfield County properties are accessed by improved dirt roads that may require advance coordination for our equipment. We discuss access requirements during the initial consultation.
  • Water quality generally excellent: Escalante area wells typically produce high-quality water with lower mineral content than Southern Utah basin wells. The Navajo Sandstone aquifer produces clean, clear water in most locations.
