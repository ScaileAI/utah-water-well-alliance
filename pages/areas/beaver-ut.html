Beaver County's Well Drilling Specialists

Beaver County is one of Utah's most agricultural counties, with ranching, farming, and rural homesteading deeply woven into the culture. Private wells are the backbone of water supply throughout the county, from the Beaver Valley floor to the surrounding highland communities of Milford and Minersville.

The Beaver Valley Groundwater Management Area is an actively managed basin with specific rules on allocation and well spacing. Agricultural wells require full water right applications with documented beneficial use. Utah Water Well Alliance knows these rules and manages the complete permit process for Beaver County projects.

Local Knowledge

Beaver County Well Facts

  • Beaver Valley Groundwater Management Area: This managed basin has specific well spacing requirements and annual allocation limits. Know your allocation before you apply — agricultural users especially need to plan ahead.
  • Variable geology: The Beaver Valley has alluvial basin fill (good for shallower wells) transitioning to volcanic and volcanic tuff formations at higher elevations. Milford-area wells often encounter older volcanic rock at depth.
  • Geothermal influences: Parts of Beaver County sit over geothermal zones. Some wells in the area encounter elevated temperatures and mineralization at depth — something to plan for in pump selection and water treatment.
  • Livestock water systems: We design and install multi-tank water systems for cattle and sheep operations — including large-capacity pump systems, float-controlled storage, and distribution piping across pastures.
