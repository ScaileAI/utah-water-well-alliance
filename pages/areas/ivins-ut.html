Ivins, Washington County

Ivins & Red Mountain Area Well Specialists

Ivins sits at the gateway to Snow Canyon State Park and the Red Mountain area — a community that has grown rapidly as one of Southern Utah's most desirable residential destinations. The spectacular red rock scenery is matched by complex geology below ground: ancient lava flows, Navajo Sandstone, and mixed volcanic intrusions create variable conditions that demand local drilling expertise.

Many Ivins properties sit on or adjacent to volcanic formations and canyon-edge terrain. Well depth and productivity can vary significantly depending on proximity to lava flows, sandstone outcrops, and the local drainage patterns of Ash Creek and associated washes. A thorough site assessment using nearby well log data is the essential first step for any Ivins well project.

Local Knowledge

Ivins & Snow Canyon Area Well Facts

  • Snow Canyon volcanic geology: The lava flows of Snow Canyon State Park extend into Ivins. Drilling through basalt requires specialized air-hammer rigs — and once through the volcanic cap, underlying Navajo Sandstone can produce excellent water yields.
  • High-end residential demands: Ivins has many premium residential properties with large lot sizes and higher water usage. We design well and pump systems sized for whole-home demand including irrigation, pools, and multiple fixtures — not just minimum domestic supply.
  • Water testing recommended: Like all Washington County wells, Ivins area groundwater should be tested for arsenic and TDS at installation and periodically thereafter. We coordinate water quality testing as part of our complete well installation service.
