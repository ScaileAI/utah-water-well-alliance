Panguitch, Garfield County

Panguitch Valley Well Experts

Panguitch sits at 6,600 feet elevation in the Panguitch Valley — the highest and coldest major valley in Southern Utah. The valley lies between the Markagunt and Paunsaugunt plateaus, which funnel snowmelt recharge into a productive alluvial aquifer that underlies much of the valley floor. This makes Panguitch Valley one of the more reliable groundwater zones in Garfield County.

The Panguitch area is predominantly agricultural — cattle ranches, hay farms, and rural homesteads that have relied on groundwater for generations. Well depths on the valley floor are typically moderate, but properties on the surrounding benches and plateaus may require drilling into limestone or sedimentary rock at greater depths. Utah Water Well Alliance understands both the valley aquifer and the surrounding upland geology.

Local Knowledge

Panguitch Valley Well Facts

  • Plateau snowmelt recharge: The Panguitch Valley aquifer is primarily recharged by snowmelt from the Markagunt and Paunsaugunt plateaus. This provides a relatively consistent and clean water source — Panguitch Valley wells generally produce good-quality water with lower mineralization than lower-elevation wells.
  • Hard winters at elevation: At 6,600 feet, Panguitch has genuine winter conditions. All wellhead equipment, pressure tanks, and above-ground piping must be properly insulated with heat tape. We build freeze protection into every installation.
  • Agricultural heritage, aging wells: Many Panguitch Valley farms have wells that are decades old. We offer well rehabilitation, pump upgrades, and redevelopment services to restore production from older wells — often more cost-effective than drilling new.
