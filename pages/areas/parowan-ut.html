Parowan, Iron County

Parowan Valley Well Experts

Parowan is one of Utah's oldest communities, settled by pioneers who understood water's value in the desert. The Parowan Valley sits at 6,000 feet elevation in northern Iron County, surrounded by the Markagunt Plateau to the east and the extensive Iron County agricultural lands to the west. Private wells are common throughout the valley, serving farms, ranches, and rural homesteads that have depended on groundwater for generations.

The Parowan area has distinct geology from Cedar City to the south — the valley fill here is shallower in places, but volcanic and limestone formations can be encountered at depth. Well yields in the Parowan Valley are generally good for domestic and moderate agricultural use.

Local Knowledge

Parowan Valley Well Facts

  • Markagunt Plateau recharge: The Parowan Valley aquifer is recharged by snowmelt from the Markagunt Plateau (Brian Head area). This makes Parowan Valley water generally clean and mineral-balanced compared to lower-elevation Southern Utah wells.
  • Cold winters: At 6,000 feet elevation, Parowan experiences cold winters. Wellhead equipment, pressure tanks, and above-ground pipe must be properly insulated and protected from freezing — something our installations account for from the start.
  • Agricultural heritage: Many Parowan Valley farms have older wells installed decades ago. We perform rehabilitation, deepening, and replacement services to bring these historic wells up to modern production levels and state compliance.
