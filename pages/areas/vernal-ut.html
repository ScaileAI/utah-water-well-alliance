Vernal & Uintah County Geology & Well Considerations

The Uinta Basin has unique geology and water quality considerations every well owner should understand.

  • Basin alluvial aquifers: Near Vernal and along the Green River, relatively productive alluvial aquifers can be accessed at 200–400 feet. Upland properties often require deeper drilling into sedimentary rock formations.
  • Water quality testing essential: Oil and gas activity in the Uinta Basin makes thorough water quality testing — including for VOCs, benzene, TDS, and methane — critical before using a new well for drinking purposes.
  • Green River drainage: Water rights in the Green River basin require compliance with both Utah DWRi regulations and Colorado River Compact allocations. Plan well in advance for new appropriations.
  • Rural access: Many Uintah County properties are remote. Our network includes drillers equipped for remote site access and self-contained operations in off-grid locations.
