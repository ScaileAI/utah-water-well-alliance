Local Knowledge

Provo & Utah County Geology & Well Considerations

Utah County's geology varies significantly from the valley floor to the foothills. Here's what property owners need to know before drilling.

  • Utah Valley Aquifer: An alluvial aquifer fed by the Provo River and Wasatch Front streams. Well depths range from 150–500 feet, with shallower wells common near the lake and deeper ones on the benches.
  • Wasatch Front geology: East bench properties encounter consolidated limestone and quartzite, requiring air-rotary drilling. Valley floor properties drill through gravel and sandy alluvial deposits.
  • Domestic exemption: Many rural Utah County properties qualify for the 0.5 acre-foot domestic exemption, simplifying the permitting process significantly for single-family use.
  • Water quality: Utah Lake's proximity affects some shallow groundwater. Testing for TDS, hardness, and bacteria is recommended for all new wells in Utah County.
