Water Well Drilling & Pump Services in Hatch, Utah

Utah Water Well Alliance serves Hatch and the surrounding Sevier River corridor of Garfield County with professional water well drilling, pump installation, and 24/7 emergency response. Situated at nearly 6,900 feet elevation along the headwaters of the Sevier River, Hatch occupies a unique position where alluvial river-corridor aquifers meet the high plateau recharge zones of the Bryce Canyon area — producing some of the most reliable and cleanest well water in Southern Utah.

Hatch, Garfield County

Sevier River Corridor Well Experts

Hatch is a small agricultural community straddling the Sevier River in the Long Valley–Bryce Canyon gateway area. The Sevier River alluvial aquifer underlying the valley floor is one of the more productive and accessible groundwater zones in the region — well depths here typically range from 150 to 400 feet, considerably shallower than many other Southern Utah communities. This makes Hatch a comparatively cost-effective area for new well construction while still delivering excellent water quality.

The community is surrounded by high plateaus — the Paunsaugunt to the east (where Bryce Canyon National Park sits) and the Markagunt to the west — that funnel substantial snowmelt recharge into the valley alluvium. This plateau-sourced recharge produces soft, low-mineral water with good natural quality. Agricultural use is central to Hatch's economy, with hay production and cattle operations relying on both surface water rights and groundwater wells. Cold winters at this elevation make freeze protection and proper well head installation a critical part of every project Utah Water Well Alliance completes in the Hatch area.

Local Knowledge

What Drillers Need to Know About Hatch

  • ▶The Sevier River alluvial aquifer is productive and relatively shallow. Unlike much of Southern Utah where drillers must penetrate hundreds of feet of hard rock, the Hatch area's Sevier River alluvium — sands, gravels, and silts deposited by millennia of river activity — can yield productive wells in the 150–400 foot range. Proper screen selection and development of gravel-pack zones in alluvial sections maximizes yield and prevents fine-sediment migration into the pump.
  • ▶Plateau recharge delivers excellent water quality — protect it with proper construction. The clean snowmelt recharge from the Paunsaugunt and Markagunt plateaus produces some of the best-quality groundwater in Garfield County. However, this clean water supply depends on maintaining the integrity of the protective aquitard layers above it. Well casing must extend through the full unsaturated zone with a proper grout seal to prevent surface contamination from agricultural activities from reaching the aquifer.
  • ▶Cold winters demand freeze protection on every installation. At 6,900 feet, Hatch experiences some of the coldest winters in Southern Utah. Hard freezes arrive by October and may persist through April. Well heads, pressure tanks, and any above-ground plumbing that is not properly insulated and heat-taped will freeze and can burst or crack during severe cold events. We design and install freeze-protected well systems as a standard practice for every project in the Hatch area — not an optional add-on.
