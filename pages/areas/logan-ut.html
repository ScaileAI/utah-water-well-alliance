Logan & Cache County Geology & Well Considerations

Cache Valley's geology and hydrology present unique opportunities and considerations for well owners.

  • Shallow alluvial aquifer: Cache Valley's deep lake sediments and river alluvium create a productive aquifer, often accessible at 100–400 feet — shallower than most of Utah.
  • Agricultural well history: Cache County has a strong tradition of irrigation wells. If you're near agricultural land, check for existing water rights on your parcel before applying.
  • Nitrate contamination: Agricultural history in Cache Valley means nitrate contamination is possible in some areas. Always test water quality before use for drinking purposes.
  • Bear River Basin: Water rights in the Bear River drainage are managed carefully. Early permit application is recommended for new wells requiring water right appropriation.
