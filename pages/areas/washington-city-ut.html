Washington City, Washington County

Washington City's Water Well Specialists

Washington City sits between St. George and Hurricane along the Virgin River corridor — one of the fastest-growing communities in all of Utah. As residential development pushes beyond municipal water service boundaries, private well demand in Washington City and its surrounding areas continues to increase steadily.

Washington City sits atop a mix of Navajo Sandstone and Virgin River alluvial deposits. The geology here differs subtly from central St. George — alluvial wells are possible in low-lying areas near the river, while upland residential developments typically require drilling into harder sandstone formations at depths of 400–700 feet. Utah Water Well Alliance has drilled extensively in the Washington City corridor and knows what each area requires.

Local Knowledge

Washington City Well Considerations

  • Rapid growth: Washington City is one of Utah's fastest-growing communities. New subdivisions frequently extend beyond the municipal water service zone, requiring private wells. Permit applications should be started as early as possible.
  • Virgin River alluvium: Properties near the Virgin River may have access to shallower alluvial groundwater. However, these shallow aquifers can be more susceptible to contamination — a good case for thorough water testing.
  • Washington County Water Conservancy District: The WCWCD manages the region's water resources and may have input on certain well permit applications. We work with WCWCD regularly and know their requirements.
