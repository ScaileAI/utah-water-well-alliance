Minersville, Beaver County

Beaver Valley Well Specialists

Minersville rests at roughly 5,500 feet elevation on the western edge of Beaver County, set against the backdrop of the Mineral Mountains and the reservoir that bears the town's name. The Beaver Valley Groundwater Management Area governs well permitting and water rights here, making proper compliance a critical part of any drilling project. Our team handles all UDWRI permit applications and GMA documentation on your behalf.

The geology west of Beaver city is a complex mix of volcanic intrusions, granite from the Mineral Mountains, and basin-fill sediments from ancient lake deposits. This mineral-rich environment often produces water with elevated hardness, iron, or manganese — all manageable with the right treatment systems. Agricultural and ranching operations dominate the landscape, and many rural homesteads depend entirely on private wells for domestic and livestock use. Utah Water Well Alliance brings the heavy-duty rotary drilling equipment and local geological knowledge needed to complete wells reliably at depths of 200 to 500 feet in this challenging terrain.

Local Knowledge

What Drillers Need to Know About Minersville

  • ▶Mineral Mountains geology drives water chemistry. The granitic and volcanic geology of the Mineral Mountains to the east frequently contributes elevated iron, manganese, and hardness to groundwater in the Minersville area. Water quality testing after drilling is not optional here — it's essential for ensuring safe domestic use and proper equipment selection.
  • ▶Beaver Valley GMA requires permit compliance before drilling. Minersville falls within the Beaver Valley Groundwater Management Area, a designated restricted area under Utah Division of Water Rights rules. All new wells require an approved permit before drilling begins, and spacing rules apply. Our team files all required paperwork with UDWRI on your timeline.
  • ▶Remote location means self-sufficiency matters. With limited municipal infrastructure serving properties outside Minersville town limits, private well reliability is a life-and-livestock necessity. We recommend annual pump inspections and water quality testing for all rural Beaver County wells, plus pressure tank sizing appropriate for irrigation and livestock demands.
